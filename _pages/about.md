I recently defended my Ph.D. and graduated from Carnegie Mellon University's School of Computer Science, where I was fortunate to be advised by [Adam Perer](https://perer.org/) in the Human–Computer Interaction Institute. Starting June 2026, I will be joining Microsoft as a Senior Applied Scientist on the Outlook Science Team, managed by Alex Williams where I will have the opportunity to expand the design space for human–AI collaboration for emails and knowledge management. During my Ph.D., my work bridged technical machine learning approaches and human-centered methods to design and evaluate explainable AI (XAI) systems that enhance human-AI collaborations in decision-making contexts, such as healthcare and wildlife conservation. Throughout my Ph.D., I completed multiple research internships at Microsoft Research and IBM Research. I graduated from the University of Pittsburgh in May 2021 with a bachelor's degree in computer science and a certificate in sustainability. In undergrad, I focused on the application and limitations of computer vision models as well as the distribution of smart mobility resources within cities.
